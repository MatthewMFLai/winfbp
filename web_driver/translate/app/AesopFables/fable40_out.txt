Fable 40

Zeus and the Oak(橡樹) Trees

Once upon a time the oak trees came to Zeus and lodged(提出（申訴，抗議等）) a complaint(抱怨；抗議). 'O Zeus, founder(創立者；奠基者) of our species and father of all plant life, if it is our destiny(命運) to be chopped(砍，劈，斬) down, why did you even cause us to grow?' Zeus(【希神】宙斯（希臘神話中的主神）) smiled and replied, 'It is you yourselves who supply the means(手段，方法；工具) of your destruction(破壞；毀滅；消滅): if you didn't create all the handles(柄，把手), no farmer would have an axe(斧) in his house!'

[There is a similar(相像的，相仿的，類似的) Greek proverb(諺語，俗語，常言), 'The ox(牛) supplies his own whip(鞭子)']
