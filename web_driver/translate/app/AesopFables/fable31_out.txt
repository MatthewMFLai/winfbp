Fable 31

The Wolves, the Sheep, and the Ram(公羊)

The wolves sent messengers(送信人；使者) to the sheep, offering(提供) to swear(發誓；宣誓) a sacred(莊嚴的；鄭重的) oath(誓言，誓約；宣誓) of everlasting(永遠的，永久的；不朽的) peace if the sheep would just agree to hand over(面交，給，傳遞) the dogs for punishment(處罰，懲罰；刑罰). It was all because of the dogs, said the wolves, that the sheep and the wolves were at war with one another. The flock(羊群) of sheep, those foolish(愚蠢的，傻的) creatures(生物；動物) who bleat(咩咩叫) at everything, were ready to send the dogs away but there was an old ram among them whose deep fleece(羊毛) shivered(發抖，打顫) and stood on end(使人毛骨悚然). 'What kind of negotiation(談判，協商) is this!' he exclaimed(呼喊；驚叫). 'How can I hope to survive(在……之後仍然生存) in your company(同伴（們）) unless we have guards(警衛)? Even now, with the dogs keeping watch, I cannot graze(（牛，羊等）吃草；放牧) in safety.'
