Fable 42

The Pine(松樹) Tree and the Wedges(楔子，三角木)

Some woodcutters(伐木工人) splitting a wild pine tree drove wedges into the trunk(樹幹), prying it apart and thus making their work easier. The pine tree groaned(（表示痛苦的）呻吟聲) and said, 'I cannot blame(責備；責怪；歸咎於) the axe(斧，斧子), who had no connection with my root, but these utterly(完全的，徹底的，十足的) despicable(令人厭惡的，可憎的) wedges are my own children. Pounded into me this way and that, they are going to tear(（被）撕開) me apart(成碎片；成零碎；破裂)!'

[The fable reminds(提醒；使想起，使記起) everyone that the bad things that strangers(陌生人) do to you are never as terrible(可怕的，嚇人的，可怖的) as the things done to you by the members(成員，一分子) of your own family.]
